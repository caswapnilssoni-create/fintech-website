How EPF Works

EPF is a mandatory retirement savings scheme for salaried employees in India, managed by EPFO. Both you and your employer contribute 12% of Basic + DA each month. The corpus compounds tax-free at a government-declared rate (currently 8.25% per annum).

Contribution Split

  • Employee 12% → entirely into EPF
  • Employer 12% → split as 3.67% to EPF + 8.33% to EPS (pension), capped at ₹1,250/month on a ₹15,000 wage ceiling

This calculator models EPF only (using the 3.67% employer share for EPF). EPS provides a separate monthly pension, not modeled here.

EPF Tax Benefits

  • Employee contribution: Deductible under Section 80C up to ₹1.5L per year (Old Regime).
  • Interest earned: Tax-free (subject to certain caps for high earners).
  • Maturity withdrawal: Tax-free if you've completed 5+ years of continuous service.
  • This makes EPF effectively EEE for most employees.

EPF Example — Age 28, ₹40,000 Basic+DA, 8% Annual Increment, 30 Years

  • Total employee contribution: ~₹65.2 lakh
  • Total employer EPF contribution (3.67% only): ~₹19.9 lakh
  • Total interest earned: ~₹1.3 Cr
  • Final corpus at age 58: ~₹2.15 Cr (EPF only, excluding EPS pension)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EPF and how does the calculator work?

EPF is a mandatory retirement scheme. Employee + employer each contribute 12% of Basic+DA. The calculator simulates year-by-year compounding at 8.25% with annual salary increments.

What is the current EPF interest rate?

8.25% per annum (FY 2024-25). Declared annually by EPFO and credited on running monthly balance.

How is employer contribution split?

12% employer = 3.67% to EPF + 8.33% to EPS (pension, capped at ₹1,250/month). Only EPF portion grows in your PF balance.

Is EPF tax-free?

Yes, if 5+ years of service completed. Less than 5 years: taxable. Interest beyond ₹2.5L/year on employee contribution is taxable per recent rules for high earners.
