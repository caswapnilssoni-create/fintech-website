How FD Interest Is Calculated

Indian banks use the compound interest formula with quarterly compounding (most common):

A = P × (1 + r/n)n × t

  • A = Maturity amount
  • P = Principal
  • r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
  • n = Compounding frequency per year (4 = quarterly)
  • t = Tenure in years

FD Tax Rules You Must Know

  • Fully taxable: FD interest is taxed at your slab rate (Old or New Regime).
  • TDS: Bank deducts 10% TDS if annual FD interest from one bank exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for seniors). 20% if no PAN. Submit Form 15G/15H if eligible.
  • Senior citizen relief: Section 80TTB allows ₹50,000 deduction on FD/savings interest (Old Regime).
  • Tax-Saver FD: 5-year FD qualifies for Section 80C up to ₹1.5L — but interest still taxable.

FD Example — ₹5,00,000 at 7% for 5 Years, Quarterly Compounding

  • Principal: ₹5,00,000
  • Annual rate: 7%, compounded quarterly (r/n = 1.75% per quarter)
  • Maturity: ₹5,00,000 × (1.0175)20 ≈ ₹7,07,389
  • Total interest: ₹2,07,389 (taxable at your slab rate)

Frequently Asked Questions

How is FD maturity calculated?

A = P × (1 + r/n)n × t with quarterly compounding (n=4) being the Indian standard.

Is FD interest taxable?

Yes, fully taxable at your slab rate in both regimes. TDS at 10% if interest exceeds ₹40K/year (₹50K seniors).

Can I save tax with FD?

Yes — 5-year Tax-Saver FD qualifies for 80C up to ₹1.5L under Old Regime. Interest still taxable.

What are current FD rates?

Major banks: 6.5-7.5%. Small Finance Banks: 7.5-9%. Post Office TD: 7-7.5%. Seniors get +0.25-0.5%.

FD vs PPF vs Debt MF?

FD: safe, taxable interest. PPF: 7.1% tax-free, 15-year. Debt MF: 7-9% market-linked, slab-rate taxed. Short-term → FD; long-term → PPF/Debt MF often better.
