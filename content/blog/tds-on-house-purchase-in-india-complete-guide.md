Buying a house is one of the biggest financial decisions in life. While purchasing a property, many buyers focus on home loans, registration charges, and stamp duty — but often forget an important tax compliance: **TDS on property purchase under Section 194-IA** of the Income Tax Act.

If you are purchasing a property worth ₹50 lakh or more during FY 2025-26, it is **mandatory to deduct TDS** before making payment to the seller.

In this article, we will explain TDS on house purchase in simple language, including applicability, rates, filing process, penalties, and important rules for FY 2025-26.

## What is TDS on Property Purchase?

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) on property purchase means the buyer has to deduct a certain percentage from the payment made to the seller and deposit it with the Income Tax Department.

This provision comes under **Section 194-IA** of the Income Tax Act. The rule helps the government track high-value property transactions and ensure proper tax compliance.

## Applicability of TDS on House Purchase

TDS is applicable when:

- Property value is **₹50 lakh or more**
- Seller is a Resident Indian
- Payment is made for:
  - Residential house
  - Flat
  - Commercial property
  - Plot or land (excluding agricultural land)

If the total property consideration is below ₹50 lakh, TDS under Section 194-IA is **not applicable**.

## TDS Rate for FY 2025-26

| Property Value | TDS Rate |
| --- | --- |
| ₹50 lakh or more | **1%** |

If the seller does not provide PAN details, higher TDS may apply as per Income Tax provisions.

## Example of TDS Calculation

Suppose you purchase a flat for **₹75 lakh**. Then:

> TDS = 1% × ₹75,00,000 = **₹75,000**

So:

- Buyer pays ₹74,25,000 to the seller
- Buyer deposits ₹75,000 with the Income Tax Department

## Who is Responsible for Deducting TDS?

The responsibility lies with **the buyer**. The buyer must:

1. Deduct TDS
2. Deposit TDS with the government
3. File Form 26QB
4. Issue Form 16B to the seller

## When Should TDS be Deducted?

TDS must be deducted:

- At the time of payment, **or**
- At the time of credit to seller's account

whichever is earlier. This includes:

- Advance payments
- Installments
- Final payment

## Due Date for TDS Payment

The buyer must deposit TDS within **30 days from the end of the month** in which TDS was deducted. Form 26QB is also filed during this process.

## How to Pay TDS on Property Purchase Online

**Step 1 — Visit TIN NSDL Website**
Open the official TDS payment portal.

**Step 2 — Select Form 26QB**
Choose: "TDS on Property (Form 26QB)"

**Step 3 — Enter Details**
Fill in:

- Buyer PAN
- Seller PAN
- Property address
- Property value
- Date of payment
- Mobile number and email ID

**Step 4 — Make Payment**
Pay TDS using:

- Net banking
- Debit card
- Bank challan

**Step 5 — Download Challan**
Save the acknowledgement receipt for future reference.

**Step 6 — Download Form 16B**
After processing, download Form 16B and provide it to the seller. Form 16B acts as the TDS certificate.

## Documents Required

You generally need:

- Buyer PAN Card
- Seller PAN Card

- Payment details

## Important Rules for FY 2025-26

### 1. TDS on Total Consideration

TDS applies on total property consideration including:

- Parking charges
- Club membership fee
- Maintenance charges
- Advance fees
- Other incidental charges

### 2. TAN is Not Required

The buyer does not need TAN for filing TDS under Section 194-IA. Only PAN is sufficient.

### 3. Multiple Buyers or Sellers

If there are multiple buyers or sellers, separate Form 26QB may be required for each combination.

### 4. Agricultural Land Exempted

No TDS is applicable on purchase of agricultural land.

## Penalty for Non-Compliance

Failure to deduct or deposit TDS may result in:

- Interest on delayed payment
- Late filing fees
- Penalty under Income Tax Act
- Notice from Income Tax Department

Therefore, timely compliance is very important.

## Benefits of Proper TDS Compliance

- Avoids legal complications
- Smooth property registration
- Prevents tax notices
- Proper tax credit to seller
- Ensures transparency in transaction

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Is TDS applicable on under-construction property?**
Yes, if the property value is ₹50 lakh or more.

**Is TDS applicable if home loan is taken?**
Yes. Even if the bank directly pays the seller, the buyer must ensure TDS deduction.

**Is TAN required for Section 194-IA?**
No, TAN is not required.

**What if property value is ₹49 lakh?**
No TDS under Section 194-IA.

**Is TDS applicable for NRI seller?**
No. Different rules under Section 195 apply for NRI transactions.

## Conclusion

TDS on house purchase is an important compliance for property buyers in India. During FY 2025-26, if the property value is ₹50 lakh or more, deduction of 1% TDS is mandatory under Section 194-IA.

Understanding the rules and completing compliance on time can help buyers avoid penalties and ensure smooth property transactions.
