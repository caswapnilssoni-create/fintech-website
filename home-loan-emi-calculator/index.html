Home Loan Tax Benefits (Old Regime)

  • Section 80C — Principal Repayment: Up to ₹1,50,000 per year (combined with PPF, ELSS, LIC etc.)
  • Section 24(b) — Interest: Up to ₹2,00,000 per year for self-occupied property. No upper limit for let-out property.
  • Section 80EE / 80EEA: First-time buyer additional benefits if loan sanctioned in eligible window.
  • Stamp duty & registration: Eligible under 80C in the year of payment.

Not available under New Tax Regime (self-occupied). Let-out interest still deductible.

Example: ₹50 Lakh Loan, 8.5%, 20 Years

  • Monthly EMI: ~₹43,391
  • Total interest: ~₹54,13,879
  • Total repayment: ~₹1.04 Cr
  • Year-1 interest benefit (24b, capped at ₹2L): ₹2,00,000 → tax saved at 30% ≈ ₹60,000
  • Year-1 principal benefit (80C, capped at ₹1.5L): ~₹70,000 (combine with other 80C items)

Frequently Asked Questions

What tax benefits does a home loan provide?

Old Regime: Principal (80C, ₹1.5L cap), Interest (Section 24(b), ₹2L cap self-occupied). First-time buyers may get 80EE/80EEA.

How is EMI calculated?

EMI = [P × r × (1+r)n] / [(1+r)n − 1], where P = principal, r = monthly rate, n = number of months.

Are tax benefits available under New Regime?

Self-occupied: No. Let-out: Interest can still be set off subject to ₹2L loss cap.
