How CTC Differs from In-Hand Salary

CTC (Cost to Company) is the headline number on your offer letter — it's the total annual cost your employer bears for you. But you don't get all of it in your bank account.

What's in CTC but NOT in-hand

  • Employer PF (12% of basic, capped) — goes to your EPF account, not your bank.
  • Gratuity (~4.81% of basic) — paid only when you leave after 5+ years.
  • Insurance premiums — health/life cover the employer pays for you.
  • ESOPs / Variable / Bonus — conditional, may not be paid every year.

What's deducted from gross to give in-hand

  • Employee PF (12% of basic, capped at ~₹21,600/year)
  • Professional Tax (~₹2,400/year in most states)
  • Income Tax (TDS) — deducted monthly by employer based on regime + declarations

Standard Salary Structure Used in This Calculator

  • Basic = 50% of CTC
  • HRA = 50% of Basic (metro) / 40% (non-metro)
  • Special Allowance = whatever's left after Basic + HRA + Employer PF + Gratuity
  • Employer PF = 12% of Basic (capped at ₹21,600/year)
  • Gratuity = 4.81% of Basic (in CTC, paid only on exit after 5+ years)

Your actual salary structure may differ — request your salary slip or offer letter breakdown for precise numbers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between CTC and in-hand?

CTC = total annual cost to employer. In-hand = bank credit after PF, Prof Tax, and TDS. Typically in-hand is 65–80% of CTC.

How is in-hand calculated?

In-hand = CTC − Employer PF − Gratuity − Employee PF − Prof Tax − TDS. The calculator uses standard 50/50/40 splits — your actual may vary.

What is professional tax?

State-level tax on salaried employees. Max ₹2,500/year (~₹208/month). Most states levy it.
